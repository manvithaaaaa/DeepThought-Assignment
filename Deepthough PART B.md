## Question 1: Sourcing Methods — How Else Would You Find Federer Companies Across India?

I spent about 30 minutes going through the 25 companies in Part A, and one pattern stood out clearly across the strongest fits — Forus Health, Remidio, Niramai, SigTuple, Tricog, Dozee. Two things were true for all of them without exception:

First, they all had **CDSCO approval** — and not just any approval. Most had Class B or Class C device approvals, the harder ones to get. A company can fake a lot of things on a website, but CDSCO licensing requires a physical manufacturing facility, documented quality processes, and regulatory inspections. It's a government-issued signal that the company is a real manufacturer, not a trader or a service provider.

Second, every Strong Fit founder came from either an **IIT/IISc background** or a **global technology company** — ex-NXP Semiconductors (Forus), ex-Xerox PARC and HP Labs (Niramai), IIT Bombay + ex-GE Healthcare (Tricog), IIT Roorkee (Dozee), IIT KGP + ex-Flipkart AI (SigTuple). Not businessmen who entered healthcare. Engineers and scientists who built something technically defensible.

These two traces — CDSCO approval and technical founder origin — are searchable at scale. That's what drives the sourcing methods below, ordered from highest signal to lowest noise:

---

### 1. CDSCO Licensed Manufacturer Registry
**What it is:** The Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation publishes state-wise lists of all licensed medical device manufacturers in India (PDF, cdsco.gov.in).

**Why it works for this ICP:** CDSCO licensing is mandatory for any company that makes regulated medical devices. A company that has gone through CDSCO licensing is, by definition, a manufacturer (not a trader), is India-based, and has met a regulatory threshold that traders/service companies cannot fake. This is the cleanest single filter for "is this actually a manufacturer?" — which is the first thing I had to verify for every company in Part A.

**How to use it:** Download state PDFs → extract company name + address → cross-reference against LinkedIn/Tracxn for promoter background and revenue.

**Limitations:** Doesn't include financial data, founder background, or growth signals. Also includes very large companies (MNC subsidiaries) and very small ones — needs secondary filtering.

---

### 2. BIRAC (Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council) Grant Beneficiaries
**What it is:** BIRAC publishes lists of companies receiving BIRAC-SEED, SBIRI, PACE, and BIG grants. Available on birac.nic.in.

**Why it works for this ICP:** BIRAC selects companies that are (a) Indian, (b) doing genuine deep tech R&D, (c) founder-operated (institutional companies don't typically apply to BIRAC), and (d) in life sciences / medical devices. A BIRAC-funded company almost always has a technical founder and is growth-oriented. The grant selection process is a pre-filter that removes commodity and non-differentiated companies.

**Limitations:** Biased toward early-stage companies. Many BIRAC beneficiaries are <Rs.30Cr — useful for pipeline, not for current Federer qualification. Use as a prospecting list, not a confirmed ICP list.

---

### 3. PLI Scheme Beneficiary Lists (Medical Devices PLI)
**What it is:** The Ministry of Chemicals & Fertilizers published lists of companies selected under the Medical Devices PLI Scheme (Rs.3,420Cr outlay). Available via DCGI and Press Information Bureau press releases.

**Why it works for this ICP:** PLI applicants are, by selection criteria: (a) Indian manufacturers, (b) investing in capacity expansion (growth mode), (c) in regulated medical device categories, (d) committed to revenue milestones. PLI selection is a government pre-certification that a company is genuinely manufacturing and growing — exactly the C6 growth signal that separated active companies from stagnant ones in my Part A research.

**Limitations:** Only ~90 companies selected under medical devices PLI; limited to specific product categories. Useful but small universe.

---

### 4. DSIR-Recognized R&D Laboratories
**What it is:** The Department of Scientific and Industrial Research publishes a list of companies with DSIR-recognized in-house R&D centers (dsir.gov.in/siro.htm).

**Why it works for this ICP:** DSIR recognition requires formal R&D infrastructure, dedicated manpower, and documented innovation output. Companies with DSIR recognition have investing R&D — a strong C3 (differentiation) signal. The list includes revenue-stage manufacturers, not just early startups.

**Limitations:** Recognition doesn't guarantee promoter-driven or correct revenue band. Still needs secondary verification.

---

### 5. India MedTech Expo / CII Medical Devices Conclave Exhibitor Lists
**What it is:** Annual government-backed expo (India MedTech Expo, organized by FICCI/CII/ASSOCHAM) publishes exhibitor lists with company names, products, and booth numbers.

**Why it works for this ICP:** Companies that exhibit at India MedTech Expo are active manufacturers investing in sales outreach — strong C6 growth signal. The expo specifically targets Indian manufacturers (not importers) and featured 500+ companies in 2025. Exhibitor list is free and public.

**Limitations:** Includes large MNCs and mid-size companies together. Also includes traders who exhibit imported products. Needs manual filtering.

---

### 6. iSPIRT / IndiaBioscience / NASSCOM Healthtech Databases
**What it is:** IndiaBioscience maintains a database of Indian life sciences companies; NASSCOM Healthtech tracks health tech startups; iSPIRT covers software products but has medtech overlap.

**Why it works for this ICP:** Community-curated databases with richer profile information than government sources. IndiaBioscience specifically tracks companies in the IIT/IISc ecosystem — the highest density of Federer-profile founders, which the Part A research confirmed.

**Limitations:** Not comprehensive; biased toward VC-funded companies which may be PE-controlled. The ICP requires promoter-driven, which is a subset of VC-funded.

---

### 7. IIT/IISc Technology Transfer Office Spin-off Lists
**What it is:** IIT Madras, IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IISc Bengaluru each maintain lists of startups incubated or spun out from their campus.

**Why it works for this ICP:** IIT/IISc spin-offs have, by definition: (a) technical founders with strong academic pedigree (C4), (b) proprietary technology (C3), (c) BIRAC/government funding (C6). The founder is typically the institute professor or a PhD student — the highest-quality Federer profile. PathShodh (IISc), Achira Labs (MIT PhD, Bengaluru-based), and Coeo Labs (IISc) in my Part A list all fit this pattern.

**Limitations:** Revenue is often small (<Rs.30Cr) for spin-offs. Many are research-stage, not yet commercial. Use as early pipeline.

---

### 8. State Industry Department (DPIIT / Karnataka Udyog Mitra) MSME Databases
**What it is:** State governments and DPIIT maintain Udyam/MSME registration databases filterable by NIC code (medical device NIC codes: 2660, 3250, 3290).

**Why it works for this ICP:** Every formal manufacturer in India should have Udyam registration. This catches the Rs.30-500Cr segment that isn't large enough to be in PLI lists but is too established for startup databases — the exact middle band where most Federer companies live.

**Limitations:** Very noisy — includes all sizes and many non-differentiated manufacturers. Requires substantial filtering.

---

### 9. Trade Associations (AIMED, FICCI Health)
**What it is:** Association of Indian Medical Device Industry (AIMED) has ~250 active member manufacturers. Member list is available on aimed.org.in.

**Why it works for this ICP:** AIMED membership is voluntary and costs money — companies that join are serious manufacturers with active industry engagement (C6 signal). AIMED specifically advocates for domestic manufacturers vs. importers — member list naturally filters for Indian manufacturers.

**Limitations:** ~250 members is a good but not comprehensive universe. Skews toward larger/established companies.

---

### If I had to prioritize

If I only had a week and no tools budget, I'd start with three: **CDSCO registry + BIRAC grant list + IIT/IISc spinoff lists.**

These three together give you ~6,000 records that have already been pre-filtered by government regulators and academic institutions for being actual Indian manufacturers doing real R&D with technical founders. Everything else — PLI lists, AIMED, expos — is additive on top of that base.

The CDSCO registry catches the manufacturers. BIRAC catches the technically differentiated ones. IIT/IISc lists catch the founder quality. That's C1, C3, and C4 — three of the six criteria — already partially answered before you've done a single Google search.

## Question 2: The 1000-Company Scale-Up Proposal

### What Part A taught me about the scale problem

Going through ~75-80 companies manually to find 25 qualified ones took roughly 30 minutes of active review for the final shortlist, with deeper background research behind it. The yield was about 30% — which matches exactly what DeepThought said to expect.

If 30% yield holds at scale, finding 1,000 qualified companies means investigating ~3,000-3,500 raw candidates in depth. Doing that manually at even 15 minutes per company is 750+ hours — not feasible for one person in a month. The plan below is built around that constraint: AI handles the volume, humans handle the judgment calls.

---

### Objective
Build a list of 1,000 ICP-qualified companies (verified as Federer profile) across India in one calendar month. Target segments: specialty medical devices + specialty biotech + custom synthesis chemicals. Quality standard: each company must have evidence on all 6 criteria with at least "Moderate" on C1 and C4.

---

### Starting Universe: Where to Source

**Primary sources** (combine for ~10,000 raw company records):
1. CDSCO Licensed Manufacturer Registry → scrape all state PDFs → extract company names + addresses → ~5,000 medical device manufacturers nationally
2. DSIR SIRO list → ~2,000 companies with in-house R&D
3. PLI scheme beneficiary lists (medical devices + pharma) → ~300 companies
4. Tracxn export or API → ~3,000 Indian medtech/biotech startups with funding data
5. BIRAC grant beneficiaries (all programs, 2015-2025) → ~800 companies
6. LinkedIn Sales Navigator "Medical Device Manufacturing India" + "Biotech India" → ~5,000 company pages
7. AIMED member list + similar trade body lists → ~500 companies

Combined de-duplicated: **~10,000-15,000 raw company records** across all segments and cities.

---

### Qualification Automation

**Stage 1 — Auto-filter (target: reduce to ~3,000 candidates)**

Using Gemini / Claude API in batch mode, process each company name through:
- Google search + LinkedIn URL
- Check for: manufacturer keywords ("manufacturing plant", "CDSCO license", "ISO 13485") vs. service keywords ("contract research", "testing lab", "analytical services")
- Revenue band estimation via Tofler API or MCA batch data
- Auto-disqualify: revenue >Rs.500Cr (via MCA), PE-majority ownership (via Tracxn cap table), "subsidiary" in company description, "imports" as primary business

Claude prompt for auto-filter (batch, ~1,000 companies/hour):
```
Given this company: {name}, website: {url}, LinkedIn: {linkedin_url}
Return JSON: {is_manufacturer: bool, revenue_band: string, auto_disqualify_reason: string or null, needs_human_review: bool}
Use these signals: CDSCO license, ISO 13485, manufacturing plant address, employee count in manufacturing.
```

**Stage 2 — ICP scoring (target: reduce to ~1,500 candidates)**

For the 3,000 surviving Stage 1 companies, run deeper AI-assisted profiling:
- Scrape company website (Claude with web search enabled)
- Scrape LinkedIn founder page
- Pull MCA financial data via Tofler API
- Score C1-C6 automatically with evidence strings

Claude prompt for scoring:
```
Company: {name}. Website content: {scraped_text}. Founder LinkedIn: {linkedin_text}. MCA revenue: {revenue}.
Score each criterion: C1 (manufacturer), C2 (India), C3 (differentiated), C4 (technical DM), C5 (growing sector), C6 (growth signals).
Return JSON with score (Strong/Moderate/Weak/NA) and one-line evidence for each. Flag if any criterion is Weak for human review.
```

**Stage 3 — Human QC (target: confirm ~1,000 qualified companies)**

From the ~1,500 AI-scored companies, human reviewers spot-check:
- All "Strong Fit" AI verdicts — sample 20% for accuracy
- All "Borderline" — review 100% for final yes/no
- All companies flagged for ownership ambiguity (PE, family group, etc.)

Expected yield: ~65-70% of Stage 2 output passes human QC → **~975-1,050 qualified companies**

---

### Week-by-Week Plan

**Week 1: Infrastructure + Universe Build**
- Set up Python pipeline: CDSCO PDF extractor, Tofler API connector, LinkedIn scraper (within ToS), Claude batch API integration
- Pull and deduplicate all source databases → raw universe of 10,000-15,000 records
- Build standardized data schema (fields match the 25-company spreadsheet format from Part A)
- Expected output: 12,000 raw records in structured CSV

**Week 2: Stage 1 Auto-filter**
- Run auto-filter Claude batch job on all 12,000 records
- Process ~1,500-2,000 records/day (Claude API rate limits allow ~1,000 web searches/hour with batching)
- Apply revenue filter (MCA data), PE/MNC filter (Tracxn cap table data), manufacturer vs. trader filter
- Expected output: ~3,000 companies surviving Stage 1

**Week 3: Stage 2 Deep Scoring**
- Run C1-C6 AI scoring on 3,000 companies
- ~300-400 deep profiles/day (requires web scraping + LinkedIn + MCA — more data per company)
- Build confidence scores (companies with 4+ "Strong" scores auto-pass; 2+ "Weak" auto-fail)
- Flag borderline cases for human review queue
- Expected output: ~1,500 companies with full AI-generated scorecards

**Week 4: Human QC + Finalization**
- Human review of 1,500 AI-scored companies (~400/day across 2-3 reviewers)
- Spot-check 20% of "Strong Fit" auto-passes for hallucinations/errors
- Review 100% of "Borderline" companies (~300)
- Add personalization hooks (Claude-generated, human-verified)
- Final deduplication, formatting, output to master spreadsheet
- Expected output: **1,000 verified ICP-qualified companies**

| Raw universe | 12,000 | — | 12,000 |

---

### Quality Control Mechanisms

**Hallucination prevention:**
- All AI-generated facts must cite a URL or source (Claude outputs `{evidence: "...", source_url: "..."}`)
- Revenue figures only accepted if MCA/Tracxn/Tofler confirm (never AI-estimated)
- Founder background only accepted if LinkedIn profile exists and is verified

**False positive prevention:**
- Any company with "CRO", "analytical services", "contract testing" in website text is auto-flagged
- Any company with 78%+ institutional shareholder is auto-flagged as PE risk
- Revenue >Rs.500Cr is hard auto-disqualify — no exceptions

**Borderline handling:**
- Companies with C4 "Moderate" (non-technical DM) are flagged — final call is human
- Companies with revenue "Unknown" are research-prioritized before inclusion
- Companies with website copyright <2022 are flagged as potentially stale
